Police Fatalities in United States

Introduction

Police Fatalities, a.k.a deaths caused by polices. Over the years, police brutality has always been a very big issue and this was one of the reasons why the movement Big Lives Matter was formed in 2013(Statista Research Department, 2021). One of the most controversial police fatalities incident was when an African-American man, George Floyd, was murdered by a police as the police knelt on Floyd's neck for over nine minutes while he was handcuffed(Wikipedia, n.d.).

In this visualization, we will be looking at the data of police fatalities throughout the years.

Number of police fatalities in the United States

In this combo map, we are able to visualize the number of police fatalities in the states of US per 1 million population along with the population of each state. We can clearly see that the state that has the darkest colour, which is Wyoming, has the highest number of police fatalities per 1 million population. It is also noticeable that some states do not have a circle plotted in it despite the state being very huge, Alaska is one of the best examples. Despite California being the state with the largest population, it also does not have the highest number of police fatalities per 1 million population.



Police Fatalities in each day throughout each month (2000-2016)

Here, we have a heatmap which showcases the number of fatalites in each day in throughout each month from 2000 to 2016. First of all, just by observing the colour of the heatmap, we can see that overall the colour of the grids from January to August have generally more darker color grid boxes. However starting from September to December, the overall color of the grid boxes are becoming lighter. Upon having this observation, we can also see the bar chart on the right which shows the sum of number of police fatalities of each month and the number of police fatalities are indeed declining starting from September. Although the reason may not be clear on why this would happen, it may be due to seasonal pattern during that time, which is Autumn.

Police Fatalities of people in different stages of life (2000-2016)

Throughout the years from 2000 to 2016, we can obviously see that regardless of which stage of life, the number of fatalities of people have been increasing gradually. Majority of the victims killed by police are adults, which is then followed by middle-aged adults, senior adults, teen. Toddler, infant and child are the minority as they would be less engaged with police in real life. We can also see that despite having the movement Black Lives Matters formed in 2013, there is no obvious or significant improve in the police fatalities. Number of police fatalities have also spiked in 2015, it is also reported that many of the victims are black men.
